What is Modern Slavery?

Modern slavery is a form of exploitation that occurs when a person is forced to work or provide services against their will. It can take many forms, including forced labor, sexual exploitation, domestic servitude, and human trafficking. Victims of modern slavery are often denied their basic human rights, such as the right to freedom, safety, and fair treatment.

Key Facts about Modern Slavery in the UK

  • Widespread Issue: The UK government estimates that there are around 13,000 victims of modern-day slavery in the country.
  • Diverse Forms: It encompasses forced labor, sexual exploitation, domestic servitude, and human trafficking.
  • Vulnerable Populations: Victims often come from vulnerable groups like children, migrants, and people with disabilities.
  • Legislation in Place: The Modern Slavery Act 2015 requires businesses to take steps to prevent slavery within their supply chains.

The Different Forms of Modern Slavery

  • Forced Labor: People are coerced into working against their will, often in dangerous or exploitative conditions. This can include factory work, agriculture, construction, and domestic work.
  • Sexual Exploitation: Individuals are forced or tricked into the sex trade, including prostitution, pornography, and forced marriage.
  • Domestic Servitude: People are trapped in domestic situations where they are forced to work without pay and are subject to control and abuse.
  • Human Trafficking: The recruitment, transportation, or harboring of people for the purpose of exploitation. This can include forced labor, sexual exploitation, and organ removal.

How can you make a Difference?

  • Raise Awareness: Talk to friends, family, and colleagues about the issue of modern-day slavery. Share information and educate others about the signs and risks.
  • Support Organizations: Donate to or volunteer with organizations that combat modern slavery, such as the Modern Slavery Helpline, Unseen, and Stop the Traffik.
  • Report Suspected Cases: If you suspect someone is a victim of modern slavery, report your concerns to the Modern Slavery Helpline on 08000 121 700 or the police.
  • Support Ethical Businesses: Choose to support businesses that demonstrate a commitment to ethical and sustainable practices, ensuring they are not contributing to modern slavery in their supply chains.
  • Advocate for Change: Contact your elected officials and urge them to support legislation and policies that effectively address modern slavery.

By taking these steps, you can contribute to the fight against modern slavery and help create a more just and equitable society.
